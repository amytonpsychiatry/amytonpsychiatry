In living life, we might often find ourselves repeating the same patterns. These patterns can lead us to suffer, be frustrated, or feel stuck. Our work together can involve understanding what these patterns are, and what parts of your life they might have been borne from. Sometimes they are derived from early childhood experiences, or later in life through significant relationships, losses, and transitions, as well as structural inequities, injustices, and marginalization. We'll investigate how these ways of living work for you, and also how they might not. I believe that by understanding how and why we live the way we do, we can then start to develop the agency to decide if we want to continue to live that way with more ease and self-compassion, or to try something new. Medications can be helpful for this process.

I can also collaborate with you and your therapist in supplementing talk therapy with medication. As part of this, we will set an intention of what we might hope to gain with the addition of medication. In the case medication is no longer helpful, we can approach safe deprescribing.

In my telepsychiatry practice, I help with trauma, anxiety, low mood, identity (particularly Asian American), and relationship struggles using a psychodynamic approach. I integrate cognitive behavioral therapy, acceptance commitment therapy, and dialectical behavioral therapy. I also practice as a hospital psychiatrist at Bellevue Hospital, where I care for individuals with psychosis, mood disorders, and trauma from a diverse range of identities, socioeconomic statuses, and backgrounds, and at NYU Langone Hospital as part of the Emergency and After Hours service.
